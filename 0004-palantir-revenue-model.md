# Palantir's Revenue Model

**Q:** How does Palantir make money / what's their revenue model?

**A:**

## Core model: Software platform subscriptions
Palantir sells access to its platforms (Foundry, Gotham, AIP) as multi-year enterprise contracts, with revenue recognized over the contract term. Two reporting segments:

- **Government** — historically larger and more stable (US defense, intelligence, allied governments, agencies). Long contracts, high switching costs.
- **Commercial** — faster-growing segment (enterprises across industries). US commercial has been the standout growth driver lately.

## How deals are priced
- **Usage/consumption + platform fees** — increasingly tied to how much the customer deploys and uses, not just flat seats.
- **Large, sticky contracts** — once an org builds its ontology and workflows on Foundry, it's deeply embedded → high net dollar retention (existing customers expand spend).

## "Land and expand" motion (central to economics)
1. **Acquire** — get in cheaply, sometimes at a loss, often via a short pilot.
2. **Expand** — once value is proven, expand to more users, departments, use cases; contract value grows.
3. **Scale** — customer becomes a large, recurring, high-margin account.

Productized entry point: **AIP Bootcamps** — short hands-on workshops converting prospects to paying customers faster, compressing the long consultant-heavy sales cycle.

## Cost/margin characteristics
- **High gross margins** (~80%), typical of software.
- Historically **GAAP-unprofitable** for years due to heavy stock-based compensation and a services-intensive forward-deployed-engineer (FDE) model. Reached **sustained GAAP profitability ~2023**.
- FDE model (embedding Palantir engineers with customers) was once a cost concern; they've made deployment more software-driven/scalable (Apollo helps).

## What's driving growth now
- **AIP** is the current catalyst — pulling in new commercial customers and expanding existing ones who want to operationalize AI on their own data.
- **US commercial** revenue and customer count growing rapidly, reducing historical dependence on lumpy government contracts.

---

**One-line summary:** Palantir makes money selling multi-year, increasingly usage-based subscriptions to its software platforms across government and commercial segments, using a "land and expand" motion (now accelerated by AIP bootcamps) to grow high-margin, sticky accounts.
